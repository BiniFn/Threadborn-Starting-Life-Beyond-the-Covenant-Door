Threadborn

Fate isn't given. It's severed.

A boy dies, wakes in Lumera, and steps into a world that was never meant to survive him.

Threadborn follows Yono Kazeshima after a sudden death throws him into Lumera beside Violet. Survival turns into dark fantasy, romance, and sealed monsters that get worse each chapter.

Volume 1: Yono learns Lumera’s rules, survives the Shade debt arc, and reaches the forest door hiding Velkor’s prison.

Yono's Powers

A simple breakdown of how Yono's power keeps climbing chapter by chapter.

From Volume 2 onward, the latest chapter is always Yono's strongest showing so far. He does not keep one final form; he turns each broken seal into the floor of the next chapter. The one limit that keeps the story alive is that he still needs someone to notice him. If nobody sees or thinks about him, he goes quiet.

Leaks

Heavy spoilers and overpowered Yono reveals. These powers are gained one by one every chapter as Yono keeps climbing higher.

Chapter-by-Chapter Unlock

These leaked powers are not dropped on Yono all at once. He gains them one by one every chapter, so each new chapter makes him stronger than the last.

Absolute Fiction Immunity

Yono becomes fully immune to any character in fiction, any outside scaling, and any attack or ability that tries to force a result on him.

Hax Immunity

Mind control, sealing, reality hacks, fate control, conceptual attacks, anti-hax powers, and all other broken cheat abilities fail on Yono unless he lets them work.

Damage Denial

At his leaked peak, damage does not register on Yono unless he personally allows the hit to count as real.

Erasure Rejection

Deletion, erasure, sealing, non-existence, and reality wipe effects all fail because Yono can refuse the rule that says he should disappear.

Rule Maker

He can create his own rules mid-fight and overwrite the rules other characters were relying on before the battle even finishes unfolding.

Any-Power Desire

Yono can use any power he desires once he decides it belongs in the scene. He does not need permission, compatibility, or a learning phase.

Power Copy x100

He can copy anyone's power with no limitation and immediately output it at one hundred times the original strength.

Pre-Attack Sight

Yono sees enemy attacks before they even happen, even on the so-called maximum speed scale, which lets him answer before the attacker's move is truly born.

Inf IQ / Inf Battle IQ

One leak gives Yono inf IQ and inf battle IQ, so he can process limitless information instantly, read the entire fight at once, and choose the perfect answer before the other side even understands the exchange.

Faster Than the Original

If an enemy starts an attack, Yono can copy that same power and use it first, before the original attacker finishes performing it.

Real-Layer Resistance

The leak version of Yono is written so that even any supposed real-world layer, author-layer, or observer-layer interference still cannot control him.

No-Loss State

Winning, losing, surviving, dying, or being countered become choices around Yono instead of outcomes forced onto him.

Past Boundless

The leak scaling places Yono past the boundless power scale itself. He is not just at the top of the chart. He is beyond the need for the chart.

Crown Contract

If someone fights Yono seriously, he can force the battle into a Crown Contract that binds the opponent to his terms.

100% Opponent Control

One leaked contract path gives Yono total control over the person fighting him, including their movement, power output, and whether they can even continue the battle.

Authority Seizure

He can take command of an enemy's powers, titles, and special forms as soon as the contract recognizes them as part of the fight.

Forced Terms

Once the contract is active, the opponent stops fighting on equal ground. They fight inside rules Yono wrote for them.

Above the Big 3

The leak scaling puts Yono beyond the strongest characters from the big 3 anime worlds by a huge margin, not by effort but by system-level superiority.

Beyond CC Goku

Leak notes place Yono above CC Goku and other extreme crossover-scale characters because Yono can reject their scaling framework entirely.

No Counter Matchups

There is no clean hard-counter matchup for the leaked version of Yono. Copy abilities, anti-hax abilities, and god-tier counters still fail.

Stronger Every New Chapter

Even these leaks are not his final cap. Each new chapter can still give Yono another power and make the newest version stronger than the last leaked version.

These leaks are full spoiler material for late-story Yono. The idea is simple: every chapter unlocks another layer, and once enough seals break he stops being measured by normal anime power systems and starts deciding the system itself.
